Eligibility Criteria

CIET certifications are open to both members and non-members who demonstrate professional competence and commitment to excellence.

üéì Educators

To apply for educator specialized certifications, candidates must:

  • Hold at least a bachelor's degree in education or a related field (higher degrees for senior certifications)
  • Be practicing educators in schools, colleges, or universities
  • Complete the relevant CIET modules for the certification
  • Demonstrate applied professional experience:
    • CPE / CCIS / CAES: 1‚Äì3 years teaching experience
    • CPML-E / CERS / CIES / CDLS-E: 3‚Äì5 years
    • CLES / CGES: 5+ years, including leadership responsibilities
  • Complete 30 CPD hours per year relevant to the certification
  • Agree to adhere to the CIET Code of Conduct and professional ethics

üíº Trainers

To apply for trainer specialized certifications, candidates must:

  • Hold at least a bachelor's degree or professional qualification relevant to training or adult learning
  • Be practicing trainers, L&D professionals, or corporate facilitators
  • Complete the relevant CIET modules for the certification
  • Demonstrate applied professional experience:
    • CPT / CWLS / CTPE: 1‚Äì3 years training experience
    • CPML-T / CLDS / CDTS / CCBTS: 3‚Äì5 years, including mentoring or project leadership
    • CLPC / COLS / CGTS: 5+ years, including strategic or global training leadership
  • Complete 30 CPD hours per year relevant to the certification
  • Agree to adhere to the CIET Code of Conduct and professional ethics

Assessment Methods

All CIET specialized certifications are awarded based on rigorous professional assessment, ensuring candidates demonstrate both theoretical knowledge and applied expertise.

1 Written Examinations

Knowledge of module content, principles, and professional standards. Typically 50‚Äì60 multiple-choice or short-answer questions, plus 1‚Äì2 scenario-based essay questions. Duration: 2‚Äì3 hours depending on certification level.

2 Portfolio Submission

Evidence of applied professional practice aligned with the certification. Includes lesson plans, training materials, curriculum examples, project reports, or mentoring records. Must include reflective practice and demonstrate impact.

3 Capstone / Applied Project

For advanced certifications, designed to demonstrate practical mastery. Can include curriculum redesign, workplace learning programs, digital learning solutions, research projects, or policy implementation. Submission includes written report and presentation.

4 Oral Presentation / Defense

For senior/executive-level certifications. Candidates present their capstone or portfolio evidence to an evaluation panel, demonstrating professional judgment, leadership, and applied expertise.

Certification Award & Re-examination

Award: Certification is awarded only when all assessments are successfully completed. Advanced certifications require both portfolio and oral defense approval.

Re-examination: Candidates who fail may re-sit exams or resubmit portfolios within 12 months. Maximum three attempts per assessment before mandatory retraining.
